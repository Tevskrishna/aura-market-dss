# RealEstateIQ — How the Product Works

A product guide for executives, mentors, and evaluators.  
You should understand the business without opening the app.

---

## The problem today

A real estate developer about to launch a project does not get one clear answer from one system.

Instead, the organisation collects pieces from:

- Sales teams  
- Marketing teams  
- Finance  
- CRM  
- Market research  
- Competition reports  
- RERA checks  
- Land acquisition  
- Excel files  
- Power BI dashboards  
- PDF packs  
- Meetings  

Every department is right in its own lane. Together, the picture conflicts.

Decision-making becomes slow. Important risks are missed. Recommendations disagree. Leadership still cannot answer, calmly and quickly:

- Should we launch?  
- Should we wait?  
- Should we change price?  
- Should we buy this land?  
- Should we increase marketing?  
- What if a competitor launches nearby?  
- What if demand softens?

**There is no single decision system.**

---

## What RealEstateIQ is

**RealEstateIQ (AURA-Market)** is a **Developer Decision Support System**.

It is not a collection of separate dashboards.  
It is one guided journey that answers one business question:

> **Should we launch this project at this price?**

The Executive Hub gives the **only final recommendation**: **GO**, **HOLD**, or **NO-GO**.

Every other module exists only to explain **why** that call is sound — market, competition, buyers, marketing, quality, project health, what-if simulation, and statistical trust — ending in a board-ready report.

---

## How RealEstateIQ solves it

1. **One project, once** — Selected on the Executive Hub and carried through the journey.  
2. **One final call** — GO / HOLD / NO-GO appears only on the Hub.  
3. **Evidence, not competing advice** — Other modules answer one business question each.  
4. **One continuous story** — Previous / Continue and Step *X* of *11* guide the executive.  
5. **One board outcome** — Reports close the journey with a decision pack, not another dashboard.

---

## Why each module exists

| Step | Module | Business question it answers |
|------|--------|------------------------------|
| 1 | **Executive Hub** | Should we launch? |
| 2 | **Market Intelligence** | Is demand healthy? |
| 3 | **Competition & Land** | Will competitors (and land cost) hurt us? |
| 4 | **Buyer Intelligence** | Who will buy? |
| 5 | **Marketing Intelligence** | Can marketing achieve the target? |
| 6 | **DMAIC Quality** | Why are problems occurring? |
| 7 | **Project Deep Dive** | Is the project financially healthy? |
| 8 | **Digital Twin** | What happens if strategy changes? |
| 9 | **Decision Explanation** | Why did the Hub decide this? |
| 10 | **SPC Control** | Can we trust this decision statistically? |
| 11 | **Reports** | What do we take to the board? |

Optional depth (Map, Demand Forecast) supports diligence when needed — they are not required to complete the main decision story.

## End-to-end workflow

**Morning (≈30 seconds)**  
Open Executive Hub → set project and ₹/sqft → read **GO / HOLD / NO-GO**, risk, business impact, AI recommendation, financial impact, confidence, and suggested actions.

**Understanding (≈3 minutes)**  
Continue through Market → Competition → Buyer → Marketing → DMAIC → Project → Twin. Each screen adds evidence for the Hub call — it does not replace it.

**Confidence (≈5 minutes)**  
Open **Decision Explanation** to see how evidence lines up behind the Hub recommendation. Check **SPC** for statistical stability.

**Close (≈10 minutes)**  
Open **Reports** → download the board decision pack. Section 0 matches today’s Hub verdict.

---

## What the user gets at the end

- A clear launch call: **GO / HOLD / NO-GO**  
- A shared understanding of **why** across market, competition, buyers, marketing, quality, and simulation  
- Confidence that the call is **statistically supportable**  
- A **board pack** that summarises the journey — ready for IC / mentor / leadership review  

---

## What this product is not

- Not a replacement for live government RERA systems (unless live feeds are configured)  
- Not twelve unrelated Streamlit dashboards  
- Not a second AI “verdict engine” competing with the Hub  

It is a **Decision Operating System** for developer leadership.
